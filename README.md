### The "Haven Quest" Engine (Browser-Based First-Person Explorer)
 * **The Platform: Spline (Again)**: This remains the core tool. Spline allows you to build interactive 3D *scenes* (a castle courtyard, a forge interior) and place your 2D character and elemental assets into that 3D space. It handles the "first-person" camera logic, collision detection (walking into walls), and event triggers (clicking on an object).
 * **The Gameplay Loop (Interactive Stewardship)**:
   1. **Environment Navigation**: The user controls a first-person camera, moving through the 3D castle or mountain grove you’ve built in Spline.
   2. **The Haven Wolf Mechanic**: As the player explores, a 3D model of your Haven Wolf (which you can create in Spline from your designs) follows them. The wolf’s state (its warmth, its hunger, its alertness) is tied to variables you set. If the wolf whimpers or stops, it acts as the *user interface*. It's telling the player they are near a "puzzle piece" or a "challenge coin" they missed.
   3. **Assembly of the Book**: Scattered throughout the environment are 3D representations of the pages of your "Kyphi" book (from your Jus-Me repository). When the player collects all the pages, they are directed to a 3D-modeled table where the book *physically assembles* in the game world, unlocking the next chapter of the lore.
   4. **Challenge Coins as Keys**: You model the challenge coin (from your Forge repository) in 3D. The player must find the coin and use it to "unlock" a specific door or chest, which then triggers a new 2D character or elemental image to appear as a reward (e.g., "You have found the Water Element!").
### Why this solves for your audience and vision
 * **High Immersion**: It uses the web as a game engine. It’s playable on any browser without downloads.
 * **Gamified Stewardship**: It forces the player to care for the wolf and the environment to progress. Ignoring the wolf leads to failure, just as you intended.
 * **Asset Flexibility**: It validates your choice to create character and elemental images, as these will be the high-value "reveals" or "rewards" for completing tasks within the 3D world.
